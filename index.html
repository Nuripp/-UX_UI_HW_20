About the Project

The project aims to redesign the Department of Energy's current website. Simplifying navigation with a clean, minimalist design and color scheme enhances its professional appearance and reliability. Emphasizing responsible web design ensures accessibility and usability across desktop, mobile and tablet versions, accommodating diverse user needs seamlessly.

Problem & Solution

What is the challenge?

The website's complex and overloaded sitemap confuses users and makes it difficult for them to find the information they need.

What is the solution?

Implementing simple and intuitive navigation with a minimalistic design and color scheme enhances the website's professional appearance and reliability

Research

Current Website

What is Department of Energy?

DOE is a U.S. government agency focused on enhancing America's security and prosperity by addressing its energy, environmental and nuclear challenges through transformative science and technology solutions.

Research Synthesis: Proto-Persona

Research Synthesis: User Path

Before usability tests, we defined user path in two ways: by clicking and by searching. We assumed that users can access the same page through different methods.

Usability Tests

5 usability tests for desktop and 2 usability tests for mobile were conducted.

What did users say? How did they feel?

"I kept ending up on different departments, which makes me confused :/"
"when clicking on the primary menu, subcategories are not appearing!"

Based on the results, 5 different categories were created and organized according to the most frequent comments made by users. The issues that are important to both users and the government agency were prioritized.

Inoformation Architecture

Card Sorting & Sitemap

Some of the primary menus and subcategories seem unrelated, and there are too many subcategories within a single menu. Through card sorting, I grouped similar items together and simplified the structure.

User Testing

User Testing Plan

1. Do users feel comfortable interacting with the website's navigations?
2. Can users easily find and access desired information or pages within the website?
3. Which device- mobile or desktop- provides a more user-friendly experience?
4. How quickly can users understand the structure of the website and locate essential functions?

Test Results

All tasks were successfully completed except for task 2.

What are the main pain points?

"I had trouble finding the home improvement page without using the menu"
"The drop down menu keeps opening automatically every time I hover over the menu, which is a bit annoying"
"Search bar isn't as noticeable compared to the other navigations"
